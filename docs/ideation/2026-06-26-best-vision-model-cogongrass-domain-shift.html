What is the very best vision model for cogongrass detection — given little data and cross-field domain shift, cost no object?

Ideation · 2026-06-26 · repo-grounded (this codebase) · Subject: the strongest vision model/approach to generalize to a new, unseen flight from limited labels.
Next step is ce-brainstorm on one direction — this doc ranks directions, it is not a plan.
Thesis (where all four research axes converge): the highest-leverage answer is not a bigger classifier, a satellite foundation model, or a zero-shot VLM. It is a frozen natural-image / plant self-supervised ViT (DINOv3 or PlantCLEF-DINOv2), continued-pretrained on your own unlabeled drone frames, wrapped with a light, per-field-refreshable head and robust test-time adaptation. "Best model, cost no object" is really "best system" — the backbone swap is necessary but the domain-shift gap closes in the self-supervised-on-your-own-imagery and adaptation layers. The single biggest structural bet is factoring "where is grass-like vegetation" (robust) from "is this cogongrass" (the hard, shifting part) via SAM 3 / visual-exemplar prompting.

Top directions (survivors)

1 DINOv3 ViT-L (frozen) + BatchNorm head — the backbone swap backbone

Why it fits Best dense/patch features of any open model; a Mar-2026 paper "DINOv3 Meets YOLO26 for Weed Detection" is direct precedent for this exact task class, and DINOv2/v3 features are repeatedly shown to hold accuracy under domain shift (species F1 0.77 vs 0.24 for a CNN under moderate shift). A frozen trunk is the intended, low-data-safe usage.
Integrate facebook/dinov3-vitl16 frozen → CLS + mean-pooled patch tokens → 2-layer MLP. Put a BatchNorm in the head so your existing AdaBN inference trick still applies (the ViT has none). Drop-in replacement for train_tiles_collection.py's ResNet18.
Tradeoffs Bespoke "DINOv3 License" (commercial-OK but conditioned — legal should read it). Start at ViT-L, not ViT-7B: with ~hundreds of labels, L wins per labeled example. The SAT-493M variant is the closest overhead-trained sibling and worth an A/B.
Sibling option: PlantCLEF-2024 DINOv2 (ViT-B/14 reg4) — DINOv2 fine-tuned on ~1.4M ground-level RGB plant photos; the best domain match of any public weights and the PlantCLEF-winning backbone. Benchmark it head-to-head with DINOv3.

2 Continued self-supervised pretraining on your unlabeled drone frames (ExPLoRA-style) method · highest leverage

Why it fits This is the move that actually drags features into your imagery manifold. ExPLoRA (2025) continues MAE/DINOv2 SSL on unlabeled target imagery (unfreeze 1–2 blocks + LoRA) and beats linear probing on aerial benchmarks at a fraction of full-finetune cost. You have the two ingredients it needs in abundance: idle DGX compute and lots of unlabeled drone frames.
Integrate One-time per imagery domain: run extra DINOv2/MAE epochs on all tiles (labels not needed), keep most blocks frozen, then this becomes the backbone everything else wraps.
Tradeoffs Highest compute of any option (hours–day) — but free here. Small unlabeled corpora can overfit; the ExPLoRA discipline (freeze most blocks) guards against it.

3 SAM 3 (concept + visual-exemplar) → frozen-feature "is-it-cogongrass" head structural reframe · highest upside

Why it fits SAM 3 (Meta, Nov 2025) does promptable concept segmentation from a noun phrase, image exemplars, or both, returning masks for all matches at once. Factoring "where is grass-like vegetation" (robust, generic) from "which grass is it" (your hard, domain-shifting decision) is the single most promising structural change for cross-field robustness — and mask area is a more honest coverage target than tile counts.
Integrate SAM 3 proposes vegetation masks → a frozen DINOv3/PlantCLEF cosine or light head classifies cogongrass vs other-grass on the masked crop. Keep the head's BatchNorm for AdaBN.
Tradeoffs New, heavier pipeline; two models at inference (fine on the Spark). Validate it beats the tile classifier on your 0606→0422 protocol before committing.

4 Few-shot adapter head + a tiny active-learning label budget per new field method

Why it fits "Expense no concern" includes a little human labeling, and 20–50 smartly chosen tiles on the new field buys more than any unsupervised trick. On frozen features, Tip-Adapter (training-free feature cache), prototype probes, or Soup-Adapters (seed-averaged, robustness for free) are far more shift-robust than full fine-tune. Pick the tiles with uncertainty × diversity (BADGE/coreset), never random.
Integrate Per new field: active-select ~30 tiles → fit a prototype/Tip-Adapter head on frozen features. LoRA-finetune only at ≥ a few hundred labels.
Tradeoffs Needs a human in the loop per field — but the cheapest, most reliable path to pushing past the 0.80–0.82 baseline.

5 Robust test-time adaptation — swap TENT → EATA / RoTTA method · fixes a current risk

Why it fits Your current TENT/AdaBN is fragile exactly in your regime: imbalanced (~88% positive), correlated, one-frame-at-a-time streams make plain TENT/CoTTA collapse (>97% error reported). RoTTA (robust BN + class-balanced memory + teacher–student) is purpose-built for correlated/label-shifted streams; EATA adds reliable-sample selection with anti-forgetting.
Integrate Keep BatchNorm in the head; replace TENT in tta_eval.py with EATA or RoTTA, update only affine/adapter params, never the frozen backbone, with class-balanced memory + reliable-sample filtering and per-frame reset caps.

6 Fourier amplitude-swap + MixStyle augmentation method · cheap win

Why it fits Amplitude carries low-level style (illumination, soil/grass color), phase carries semantics — swapping amplitude synthesizes "new-field" styles while preserving the grass structure. The single best-matched augmentation to a cross-field illumination/color shift, ~zero extra cost, stacks on your existing CLAHE + domain-randomization.
Tradeoffs Can wash out the green-ness cue your ExG filter relies on — tune perturbation strength.

7 Dark horses — C-RADIOv3 backbone & T-Rex2/YOLOE exemplar detector alt framings

C-RADIOv3 (NVIDIA): one ViT distilled from DINOv3-7B + SigLIP2 + SAM3 teachers, explicitly engineered for multi-domain robustness ("Reduce All Domains Into One") and DGX/ARM64-native. Pick it if the DINOv3 license is a blocker or you want robustness + semantics in one backbone.
T-Rex2 / YOLOE (visual-exemplar one-shot detection): give 3–10 example crops of cogongrass per new field as the only "labels"; strongest published evidence on custom-domain (Roboflow100/ODinW) generalization — the closest proxy to "a new flight." Lower precision than your tuned classifier on the trained field; best as an exemplar-prompted region proposer feeding your head.

Rejected — and why (the value is in the explicit "no")

CandidateWhy rejected for this problem
Satellite/EO foundation models (Prithvi-EO-2.0, Clay, SatMAE/Scale-MAE, DOFA, Galileo, AnySat) Pretrained on nadir, multispectral satellite imagery; several bake top-down geometry or GSD positional encodings into the architecture. Your data is oblique, RGB-only, single-frame — their entire advantage (spectral bands, satellite GSD, temporal stacks) is unavailable. Honest verdict from the geospatial scout: poor fit. (RGB DINOv3-SAT is the only EO-adjacent option worth a sanity A/B.)
Large VLM as the classifier (Qwen2.5-VL, InternVL, Gemini, GPT-4o) 2025–26 agronomy benchmarks: supervised YOLO/CNN substantially beats every zero-shot VLM; best (Gemini-3 Pro) ~62% on multiple-choice, <25% open-ended, with documented reasoning-driven hallucination and poor calibration, and no cross-domain testing. This is exactly why the in-flight Qwen2-VL-7B run is a capability read, not a deployment path. Keep VLMs for explanation + hard-negative mining only.
Text-only open-vocab detection ("cogongrass" → Grounding DINO) Text prompts won't reliably localize a fine-grained species; generic "grass" cues dominate. Needs visual exemplars (→ T-Rex2/YOLOE/SAM 3), not a label string.
Plain TENT / CoTTA at test time Collapses under imbalanced, correlated, small-batch streams — your exact regime. Superseded by EATA/RoTTA.
EVA-02 · V-JEPA 2 · Hiera/MAE backbones EVA's headline numbers are supervised-IN fine-tuning, not label-free transfer; V-JEPA is a video model (wrong tool for static tiles); Hiera/MAE sit below DINOv2/v3 on frozen linear-probe transfer.

If expense is truly no concern — the ceiling system

Stack the survivors rather than pick one: DINOv3-L (or PlantCLEF-DINOv2), continued-SSL on your full unlabeled drone corpus, used inside a SAM 3 where/what factorization, with a few-shot adapter head refreshed per field from ~30 active-learning labels, EATA/RoTTA at inference, and Fourier/MixStyle augmentation at train time. Validate every layer against the existing 0606→0422 cross-collection protocol — that honest held-out number, not benchmark headlines, decides what beats the 0.80–0.82 baseline.
